This interview was conducted by myself and Pari Sachdev with Dr. Lindy Elkins-Tanton, the Space Sciences Laboratory Director, Principal Investigator of the NASA Psyche mission, and Earth and Planetary Sciences Professor at UC Berkeley. In this interview, Dr. Lindy discussed some of the top-priority science goals of our first missions to Mars, as part of her work on the A Science Strategy For the Human Exploration of Mars committee. She also discusses what awaits with the Psyche asteroid as the spacecraft approaches it in 2029. Space exploration has benefited society in many unique ways, and helps us work together, as Dr. Lindy reflects on Artemis II, Psyche, and more.
